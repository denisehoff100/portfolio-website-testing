My first job was with McGraw-Hill in NYC. I learned to collaborate with a fantastic group of creative people—I learned by doing. Since then, my work has allowed me to work and travel to different countries around the world: India, Singapore, Hong Kong, Dubai, etc. Living in different cultures has offered me a unique perspective when designing products to meet the needs of users. Most recently, as a teacher, I gained insight on how our work as designers must adapt to meet the various needs of our users.

Fun Facts

Early in my career, I played on multiple Broadway shows and Carnegie Hall. Before that, stitched costumes for Phantom of the Opera!

I moved my family to India to lead an offshore design & production studio of 100 people to augment our US studio.

How in the world did this happen?

While working at McGraw-Hill I took on extra freelancing projects. One client led to many more, and before I knew it, I had a company which grew from a small design and production studio in NYC/FL to a larger facility in India. Rather than outsourcing, we became the ‘outsourcer’.

Whew!

Lots of stress overseeing a large team across the ocean! Through that experience I realized that people, no matter what country you are in, all strive for the same thing in life. Purpose.

14 years later, I chose to return to music, as a musical director and teacher at a prep school. Throughout that entire time I kept my hand in design. One positive result of covid was that I had time to take the plunge and embark on a UI/UX Career Foundry boot-camp course of 500+ hours, bringing me full-circle back to what I loved at the start of my career, visual design.

One positive thing that happened during this hibernation-year of covid, is that I even learned enough HTML/CSS to code this site from scratch!
