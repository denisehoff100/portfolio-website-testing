My first job was with McGraw-Hill in NYC. I learned to collaborate with a fantastic group of creative people—I learned by doing. Since then, my work has allowed me to work and travel to different countries around the world: India, Singapore, Hong Kong, Dubai, etc. Living in different cultures has offered me a unique perspective when designing products to meet the needs of users. Most recently, as a teacher, I gained insight on how our work as designers must adapt to meet the various needs of our users.

Fun Facts


Years ago, I played in the pit on Broadway. And, before that, stitched costumes for Phantom of the Opera!

Eventually I decided to try freelancing. One client brought many more, and before I knew it, I had a company which grew from a small design and production studio in NYC/FL to a larger facility in India. Rather than outsourcing, we became the ‘outsourcer’. Whew! Lots of stress overseeing a large team across the ocean! Through that experience I realized that people, no matter what country you are in, all strive for the same thing in life. Purpose. After 14 years I chose to return to music, as a musical director and teacher at a prep school. I continued to keep my hand in design, and recently realized that my true calling is that of a designer. So, I took the plunge and embarked on a boot camp course bringing me full-circle, back to what I loved at the start of my career, visual design.
